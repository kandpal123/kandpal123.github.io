About Me

I am currently working as a Theme Expert (Remote Sensing & GIS) in Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (MoA&FW), Government of India. My research interest includes Hyperspectral Remote Sensing, Agriculture, Ecological monitoring, Spectral libraries, Plants biochemical and biophysical parameters, Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL). My research focuses on leveraging NextGen remote sensing technologies and Artificial Intelligence (AI) approaches to decode the complex chemical and biophysical parameters of vegetation. The integration of these fields promises to optimize resource use, enhance crop yields, and minimize environmental impact. My academic journey has been rooted in the intersection of environmental science, geospatial technologies, agronomy, and data science. During my doctoral studies at the Academy of Scientific and Innovative Research (AcSIR), CSIR-IHBT (Institute of Himalayan Bioresource Technology), India, I specialized in the development of deep learning models for vegetation parameter retrieval. Specifically, my research focused on rapid and non-invasive tools and techniques for complex growth and development parameters such as plant age, plant type, and secondary metabolites (minute chemical constituents of plants) with the implementation of next-generation remote sensing technology and artificial intelligence approaches. Till now, these variables have not yet been explored in the prediction of these variables in a non-destructive manner. Apart from these outcomes, I have also developed a technology for non-destructive estimation of chlorophyll contents in the fresh leaves of plant species using Hyperspectral reflectance data. I also have experience in surveying for spectral data recording of medicinal plants and ground truthing in the high-altitude meadows of the Himalayan states in India.
